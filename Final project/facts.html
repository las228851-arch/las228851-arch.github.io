Where Capybaras Live

Capybaras are native to South America and are usually found near rivers, lakes, ponds, and wetlands. They prefer warm environments where they can easily swim and cool themselves in water. Countries such as Brazil, Venezuela, Colombia, and Argentina are home to many wild capybaras. Because they spend so much time near water, capybaras are excellent swimmers. Their eyes, ears, and noses are located near the top of their heads, which helps them stay mostly underwater while remaining alert.

Capybaras are very social animals and often live in groups ranging from ten to twenty members. During dry seasons, even larger groups may gather around water sources. Living in groups helps capybaras stay safe from predators. They communicate using whistles, barks, clicks, and body movements. Young capybaras learn social behavior by staying close to adult members of the group. Their peaceful nature allows them to live comfortably with many other animals.

Many people are surprised to learn that capybaras are actually rodents. In fact, they are the largest rodents in the world. Adult capybaras can weigh more than one hundred pounds and grow over four feet long. Despite their large size, they are herbivores and mainly eat grass and aquatic plants. Their teeth continuously grow throughout their lives, similar to other rodents. They spend a large amount of time eating in order to maintain their energy.

Quick Facts

  • Largest rodent in the world
  • Can swim underwater for several minutes
  • Lives in South America
  • Very social and peaceful animals

Capybaras are famous online because they seem calm in almost every situation. Videos often show them relaxing peacefully with birds, monkeys, cats, and other animals. Their relaxed behavior has made them symbols of calmness and positivity around the world.
